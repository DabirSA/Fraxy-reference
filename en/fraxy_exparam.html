Parameter 1 has the following possible values:
0: Parameters 2~4 and 16 have no effect.
~-1: Fade-type speed control. The value you enter here should be the negative of the fade type you want, ie -14 gets fade type 14. Refer to the fade type list in the Misc window of the editor.
1~: Sine wave-type speed control. The value you enter here controls the number of half-sine waves the bullet's speed goes through over its lifetime.
Examples:
1: The bullet starts at initial speed, then accelerates to final speed.
2: The bullet starts at initial speed, accelerates to final speed, then decelerates to initial speed.
3: The bullet starts at initial speed, accelerates to final speed, decelerates to initial speed, then accelerates to final speed again.

Parameter 5 has the following possible values:
0: Parameters 5~6 have no effect.
1~: Each shot from the part fires this many projectiles in a spread pattern.

Parameter 6 sets the angle between the front of the part and the outermost projectiles of the spread described in parameter 5.
The other projectiles in the spread are spaced out evenly within this angle range.

Parameter 8 has the following possible values:
0: Projectiles have their defaut lifetime.
1~: Projectiles last this many frames before disappearing.

If parameters 14 and 15 are set, projectiles will change direction once during their lifetime.

Parameter 14 has the following possible values:
-1: Projectiles will aim towards the player.
0: Projectiles will not change direction.
1~4095: Projectiles will alter their direction by this much.
Examples:
1024: Projectiles will turn 90 degrees right
2048: Projectiles will start travelling backwards
3072: Projectiles will turn 90 degrees left

Parameter 15 has the following possible values:
0: Projectiles will not change direction.
1~: Projectiles will change their direction after this many frames have passed.

Parameter 16 has the following possible values:
0: Projectiles rotate normally
1: Projectiles rotate towards their original firing direction (only applies to projectiles fired in a spread shot by params 5~6)
2: Projectiles rotate to home in on the player
&8: Rotation speed fades out over time
&16: Rotation speed fades in over time
&32: Rotation speed is controlled by fade type 24

Note that use of parameter 1 enables several other exparams which default to 0.
You must manually set those exparams in order for the weapon to function, including initial speed (param 2), final speed (param 3) and self-destruct timer (param 8).

Parameters 4 and 11 define a set of ratios of time spent between the three stages of speed.
For example, 104050 -> 10:40:50. The missile spends 10% of its life at its initial speed, 40% of its life accelerating to final speed, and 50% of its life at its final speed.
Entering 010405, 020810, 041620 etc would get the same result.
Mon originally intended to make this hexadecimal, but found that it was awkward to work with.
If you use decimal-unfriendly numbers, you may get unexpected results. For instance, 010101 will result in ratios of 33:33:34. Stick to numbers that can easily be resolved into percentages.

Parameters 13-14 define a looping cycle of contraction and expansion.
Parameter 13 defines the percentage of maximum size that it reduces by at its minimum size.
For example, if you set maximum size to 200, and set parameter 13 to 30, the rings will cycle between 200% and 140%.
If you set parameter 13 to a negative, the rings will follow the same cycle as if it were positive, but they will start at their smallest size instead of their largest size.
In the above example, if you set parameter 13 to -30 instead of 30, the rings will cycle between 140% and 200%.
Parameter 14 defines the period of the cycle, meaning the time it takes the ring to contract to minimum size and expand back to maximum size again.
